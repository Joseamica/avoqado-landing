# Impeccable Design Context — Avoqado Landing

## Design Context

### Users
Venue owners and operators across Mexico — restaurants, hotels, gyms, retail stores, and entertainment businesses. They are business-focused, not technical. They visit avoqado.io to evaluate a payments and management platform. Their context: they're busy, often on mobile, comparing options, and need to quickly understand what Avoqado does and why it's better than their current setup.

### Brand Personality
**Modern, Premium, Approachable**

- **Modern**: Dark-first aesthetic, scroll-driven animations, cinematic product reveals. Feels like the future of venue management.
- **Premium**: Stripe/Square-tier polish. Every pixel earns trust. No shortcuts, no stock photos, no template energy.
- **Approachable**: Warm enough for a restaurant owner who isn't technical. Clear language (Spanish), intuitive hierarchy, never intimidating.

### Emotional Goals
1. **Excitement & possibility** — "This will transform how I run my business"
2. **Confidence & trust** — "This is serious, reliable technology"
3. **Relief & simplicity** — "Finally, something that just works"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode primary with OKLCH green-tinted neutrals (hue 155). Brand green `oklch(0.78 0.18 155)` as the accent of energy and action. Full palette in `global.css` with 5 green shades, 11 tinted neutrals, 4 surface elevations, 4 semantic colors, and 4 product accents.
- **Visual tone**: Clean fintech meets hospitality warmth. Think Square's clarity + Stripe's information hierarchy
- **Typography**: DM Sans for everything — headings (light/medium weight, large sizes) and body (regular weight). Playfair Display italic for rare editorial accents only. BabyDoll reserved exclusively for the Avoqado logo/brand mark, never for page headings.
- **Motion**: Scroll-linked animations (Framer Motion) — cinematic, purposeful, never gratuitous. Every animation reveals information or builds narrative
- **Photography**: Real venues, real people, real products. Diverse Mexican businesses. No stock photos.
- **Layout**: Generous whitespace (on dark), sticky scroll sections, grid-based compositions

### References
- **Square / Toast**: Clean POS-focused fintech aesthetic, product-forward
- **Stripe / Wise**: Premium fintech with clear information hierarchy, trust signals

### Anti-References (What Avoqado Must NOT Look Like)
- Generic SaaS templates with stock photos and cookie-cutter layouts
- Enterprise/corporate software — stiff, formal, blue-gray
- Overly playful/cartoon — no mascots, no childish illustrations
- Cluttered/information-heavy — no walls of text, no feature comparison tables

### Design Principles
1. **Show, don't tell** — Demonstrate the product through animation and real imagery rather than describing features in text blocks. Implement visual alternatives, never just describe them.
2. **Cinematic scroll** — Use scroll-linked animations to build narrative. Each section is a scene that reveals on scroll. Sticky viewports, staggered reveals, purposeful motion.
3. **Premium simplicity** — Every element earns its place. Generous spacing, clear hierarchy, no clutter. If it doesn't serve the user's decision, remove it.
4. **Trust through craft** — Polish is the trust signal. Pixel-perfect alignment, smooth 60fps animations, consistent design tokens. The quality of the site reflects the quality of the product.
5. **Accessible premium** — Dark mode done right. WCAG-compliant contrast ratios, focus-visible styles, reduced-motion support. Premium doesn't mean exclusionary.

### Technical Constraints
- Astro Islands Architecture — React components hydrate only when needed (`client:visible`, `client:idle`, `client:load`)
- Tailwind CSS 4.x with custom theme tokens defined in `global.css`
- Framer Motion for scroll-linked animations (useScroll + useTransform pattern)
- Mobile-first responsive design — every change must work across mobile/tablet/desktop
- Spanish language (Mexican market)
- No emojis in code, UI, or content
